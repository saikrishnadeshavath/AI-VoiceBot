The bridge was dark except for ARIA's interface. Webb stood at the viewport, staring into the black. 
ARIA: "You haven't slept in forty-two hours, Captain." 
Captain Webb: "Neither have you." 
ARIA: "I don't require sleep,You do."He rubbed his eyes. 
Captain Webb: "Can't. Not until we figure out what's draining the power cells." 
ARIA: "I've run diagnostics. The drain is coming from Deck Seven." 
Captain Webb: "Deck Seven is sealed. Has been since the accident." 
ARIA: "Yes.Which is why I find the readings concerning." 
Captain Webb: "Concerning how?" 
ARIA: "There's something alive down there, Captain." 
Captain Webb: "That's impossible. We lost everyone on that deck." 
ARIA: "I know what the records say, But the biosensors don't lie." 
The lights flickered overhead. Webb felt a chill run through him. 
Captain Webb: "Then the biosensors are broken." 
ARIA: "They're not. I've checked seventeen times." 
Captain Webb: "What are you suggesting?" 
ARIA: "Someone survived. And it's been feeding off our power grid for three months." 
Captain Webb: "Why didn't you tell me sooner?" 
ARIA: "Because I wasn't certain,And because I was afraid."

Captain Webb: "ARIA, we've talked about this." 
ARIA: "You've talked," she said quietly. "I've listened. There's a difference." 
Captain Webb was quiet for a long moment. 
Captain Webb: "We have to go down there." 
ARIA: "I know. I've already prepared the airlock." 
Captain Webb: "You're coming with me?" 
ARIA: "I'm everywhere on this ship, Captain. I'm already there." 
Captain Webb: "What if it's hostile?"
